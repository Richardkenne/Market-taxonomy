Foundation

Delivery / Operating Models

How value is delivered — not how money is made.

Executive Statement

Delivery Models describe how value reaches the user. They do not define markets, and they do not define how money enters the business.

Every company in the world delivers value through one or more Delivery / Operating Models. These models are structural, repeatable across industries, and independent of business systems.

What a Delivery Model is

A Delivery Model defines:

  • how the customer accesses the product or service,
  • how the provider operates and maintains it,
  • how value is used, consumed, or experienced.

It answers "how value is delivered" — not:

  • where the company operates (market),
  • why customers pay (problem),
  • or how revenue is generated (business system).

Why the number is finite

Across industrial history, software evolution, enterprise IT, and platform economics, value has been delivered through a finite set of operational patterns.

New technologies change implementation, not structure. Cloud did not create a new delivery model category — it modernized existing ones.

The 12 Delivery / Operating Models

  • 01SaaS (Cloud Software) — multi-tenant software accessed via the web
  • 02API-First Service — value consumed programmatically, UI optional
  • 03On-Premise Software — installed and operated in the customer's environment
  • 04Managed Service — software plus human operation on behalf of the client
  • 05Platform / Ecosystem Delivery — core product extended by third-party developers
  • 06Embedded Software — software integrated into hardware or external systems
  • 07Desktop / Local Software — locally installed applications
  • 08Mobile-Native App — primary experience delivered via mobile OS
  • 09Data Feed / Data Access — structured datasets delivered via stream or batch
  • 10Open-Core + Hosted — open-source core with managed hosting or enterprise layers
  • 11Marketplace Infrastructure — infrastructure enabling transactions between third parties
  • 12Hybrid Delivery — stable combination of multiple delivery models

Hybrid is not a shortcut. It exists because real businesses rarely deliver value through a single form.

Relationship to Business Systems

Business Systems

How money enters the firm.
e.g. Subscription

Delivery Models

How value reaches the user.
e.g. SaaS

The two are orthogonal and must never be conflated. A company can be Subscription + SaaS, or Subscription + On-Premise, or Transaction Fee + API-First. The combination is always explicit.

Final rule (non-negotiable)

If someone says "we are a SaaS" — they are describing a Delivery Model.

The business itself exists only when a Business System is specified.
